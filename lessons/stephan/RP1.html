3 Summary - 5 minutes

To summarize this lesson, I am going to ask students to have a Table Discussion on the following questions: 

  1. What is the purpose of a unit rate? 
  2. How are unit rates useful in making comparisons? 

While tables discuss I will walk around and listen in, and then I will have tables share out.  This activity will give me a good sense of if students get the big picture of unit rates.

Table Discussion Reflection
Checks for Understanding

The table discussion for this lesson really wrapped things up and helped me gauge the students' level of understanding.  All students discussed the importance of unit rates and why they are used to make comparisons, and I had one lucky table share out to the whole group.  They shared that "unit rates are important because they provide equality with the units" - which I thought was a great answer for 7th graders!  They went on to say that without the unit rate they thought that one person in their group was the best hopper, but once they calculated the unit rate they realized they were wrong!  I think the hopping lab really helped them to understand the importance of a unit rate.
